Our Brand


Vision

To build a collective of like minded artists that spread the word of equality through fun.


Promise

We will expand our knowledge to improve the games that encourage all of us to become better humans.


While our company is still new to the industry, our unique mindset on the inclusion gives our team the drive it takes to become a substancial competitor in the gaming market.









Core Values

Passion:

The drive that inspires and pushes us to think beyond the boundaries of what is possible.


Absolute Equality:

The belief that every person, no matter their background, deserve to be treated as a person.


Continuous Learning:

The need to explore outside of our comfort zones in order to produce the best of the best.


Communication:

The ability to be honest with each other and openly express needs from person to person.


Kindness and respect:

The power to encourage each other with good actions, understanding words, and genuine admiration for each other.



Brand Identity

Working together is not always easy, but with very challenge met a new clarity is formed. As people we have disagreement, creative differences, and natural opposition. Instead of shunning each other's different perspective we use them to grow into something more powerful.

Our clients are the company's niblings, and we are the aunts, uncles and titis here to guide and teach important life lessons. Learning from peers and parents will only gain you so much knowledge, but through the power of our creations we hope to expand the minds of today's youth.

We believe that positive communication is most direct path to a stable work environment. We don't discriminate against any persons for their level of knowledge, personal identity, or their birth given attributes. This doesn't stop us from weeding out those who don't abide by our team player perspective. Each person is given ample opportunity to show their drive, while simultaneously being given the space they need to thrive.



Logo

Do's

The logo should always strive to be as large as possible on each medium it is used on. The minimum size is a 100 pt width. The logo should always go in the bottom left corner. If this is not available it can be placed in the top left corner.


To prevent anything from interfering with the impact of the logo, there should be a space of 50pt surrounding the logo. These sizes can be scaled up for larger projects but should maintain the same proportion.


The color version of the logo is preffered across all mediums, but in the event that these colors cannot be properly achieved on a project either of the black and white versions here are acceptable. Use them sparringly and only when absolutely required.

Background Shapes

Background shapes are used in conjunction with icons and colors to create dinamic movement and eye direction.


Shapes are large scale, simplistic and echoe the elements found in the logo. This includes the rounded edges of the blue square, the slanted corner of the âTâ, and the winding shape of the âSâ. Any of these elements can be combined with one another or simply used as inspiration for background shapes.


Shapes can be duplicated and stacked to give a more three dimensional look to them. Each shape should utilize solid colors without gradient and no outline. Shapes can be pointed in any direction (horizontal, diagonal, vertical). Shapes can be used to form a pattern that has elements of the same size and shape repeated evenly across the design. If there is a repeating pattern, there can be no more than 2 colors shown in the pattern.


Do Not's

While the logo maintains some versitility, there are a varity of prohibitations when it comes to altering the logo.


The logo should not be stretched or squashed in any way; this includes individual elements, such as the apostraphy.


The shape and placement of the letters and outter rounded square should not be moved or adjusted.


The colors within the logo should not be changed except to those that were previously shown.


No shadows can be added behind, or images covering the front of the logo.

Icons

Icons are used to accent the overall design and direct the eye to relevant points of interest. They bring a playfulness to the content in a subtle way. Use them sparingly.


This elements can be used in the background or foreground of a design. Maximum number of colors for any icon is 2. Icons do not have outlines, shadows, and should not be double stacked.


To the left are several examples of approved icons. They are prodominently one color with transparent segments shown in small amounts. These can be replaced with color instead of being transparent. The colors chosen cannot be black and white.



Graphics


Graphics include photographs, game screenshots, and other assorted pictures that are not icons. They are used to support the verbal content of a design. In many cases, the content may be the graphic while the words around it are the supporting element.


Photographs should be supported by other elements, such as icons and background shapes, in some way. Graphics can also be used in conjuntion with the background shapes by filling in the space of the shape instead of a color.


Be mindful of the colors within an image selected, and be sure they fit the theme of your design.
